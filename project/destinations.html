Explore Destinations

Boracay

Located in the central Visayas region, about an hour’s flight from Manila City, Boracay Island is home to what is widely regarded as the best tourist spot in the Philippines—White Beach. Stretching for 4 kilometers, White Beach boasts powdery, pure white sand that feels like walking on clouds, making it one of the finest beaches not only in the country but also in the world.

Nacpan

Perfect for a relaxing beach day, Nacpan Beach invites you to unwind in one of the most picturesque settings in Palawan Island. Just steps away, you'll find Calitang Beach, and together they form the iconic Twin Beaches of El Nido town, a sight that’s as breathtaking from the shore as it is from above.

Siargao

Siargao Island, located in the island region of Mindanao, south of the Philippines, has rapidly become one of the country's top destinations, especially for surf enthusiasts. Known as the country’s surfing capital and even considered by some as the surfing mecca of Asia, Siargao has transformed from a quiet island into a hotspot for adventure seekers and young travelers.

Tubbataha

Tubbataha Reef, one of the top diving spots in the Philippines and the world, is located in the heart of the Sulu Sea, off the coast of Palawan Island. This UNESCO World Heritage site is renowned as a center of marine biodiversity, attracting divers and marine conservationists alike.

Intramuros

Step back in time and explore the storied streets of Intramuros, the iconic walled city and one of the Philippines' most treasured historical landmarks. This 64-hectare Spanish stronghold, built by conquistador Miguel Lopez de Legazpi, stood resilient for centuries until it was ravaged by World War II. Yet, its captivating ruins remain, offering visitors a glimpse into the grandeur of old Manila City.

Vigan

Located about 400 kilometers north of the country's capital, Manila City, the historic city of Vigan in the province of Ilocos Sur transports you to the 16th century with its old-world charm and beautifully preserved Spanish colonial architecture.As one of the UNESCO World Heritage Sites in the Philippines, Vigan City is a must-visit, with Calle Crisologo being one of the crown jewels of this historic town in Ilocos Sur province.

Kawasan

Cebu Island is also a hub for adrenaline-pumping activities, and Kawasan Falls is the grand finale of the island’s famous canyoneering experience. This tour is packed with excitement as you swim through rivers, rappel down rugged rock faces, and leap from mini-waterfalls—culminating in a refreshing plunge into Kawasan Falls’ cool, crystal-clear waters.

Kayangan

Located in the northern part of Palawan Island, the town of Coron is a coastal paradise in the Philippines known for its breathtaking natural beauty. Among its many gems, Kayangan Lake stands out as a must-visit destination. Often hailed as the cleanest lake in the Philippines and Asia, Kayangan Lake’s crystal-clear waters and surrounding limestone cliffs create a magical setting that you can visit by joining island hopping adventures.

Underground River

Puerto Princesa City serves as the gateway to Palawan Island and is home to the world-renowned Puerto Princesa Underground River, a UNESCO World Heritage Site and one of the New 7 Wonders of the World. Spanning over 8 kilometers, it is the longest underground river in the world that can be explored, winding through a magnificent cave system adorned with impressive stalactites and stalagmites.
